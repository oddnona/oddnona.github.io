The Life of a Plastic Particle

3D Data Visualization of Marine Plastic Transport

Bachelor's thesis project investigating the use of immersive 3D visualization as a tool for communicating scientific data about marine plastic pollution.

Video Demonstration

Abstract

Marine plastic pollution poses a global threat. Large plastic objects discarded at the ocean surface fragment into increasingly smaller particles which eventually settle in deeper ocean layers. Recent scientific findings highlight the hazards these microplastics and nanoplastics pose to marine ecosystems, food supplies, and human health.

Because microscopic particles are difficult to imagine, the general public often struggles to perceive the urgency of these threats. This project explored whether immersive scientific visualization could improve understanding of marine plastic behavior.

The project implemented a narrative-driven 3D visualization of nanoplastic trajectories inside a virtual environment, depicting the journey of an average particle from surface drift to deeper ocean layers.

A user study was conducted to evaluate comprehension and attitudes toward the subject. Results indicated improved conceptual understanding of marine plastic behavior, although communicating precise quantitative information and dynamic processes remained challenging.

Key Contributions

  • Interactive 3D scientific visualization
  • Narrative-driven educational experience
  • Visualization of nanoplastic transport data
  • User study evaluating comprehension and engagement
  • Research on science communication through immersive media

Technologies Used

  • Unity
  • C#
  • 3D modelling and asset integration
  • Scientific data visualization techniques
  • User study design and analysis

Results

Participants reported high levels of engagement and showed improved conceptual understanding of marine plastic transport and transformation processes.

While the visualization effectively communicated overall concepts and environmental impact, conveying precise quantitative information remained a challenge.
